Implementation: Team Accessibility


First time participants at the Code for SF Hack Night could feel reluctant to approach project teams either due to unclear locations of project teams or the concerns of disrupting team work . We decided to implement the three programs below to improve team accessibility: project team shout-out at Hack Night orientation, visible team name display, and team ambassador program..


Project Shout-out at Hack Night Orientation

Project leads gave a 30-second pitch about project mission statement and project needs at the orientation. New members could gain basic knowledge of the project teams at the Hack Night, identify their projects of interest, and initiate conversations after the orientation.


Visible Team Name Display

Project teams are encouraged put up their names on a metal stand, or on the door/wall, right around where the teams gather. For new members that have identified projects of interest either online or at the orientation could more easily navigate the space and find the teams that they want to work with.


Team Ambassador Program

Each project would appoint 1-2 team ambassadors responsible for interacting with new members at the Hack Night. Team ambassadors are made identifiable to new members with their name tags on the arm, instead of on their chest. And new members should feel free to approach any team ambassadors without concerning about disrupting work.
