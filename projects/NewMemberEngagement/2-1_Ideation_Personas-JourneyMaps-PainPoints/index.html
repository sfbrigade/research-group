Ideation: Personas & Journey Maps


In the ideation phase, we first aimed to create personas that represent Code for SF new members and journey maps that capture their experiences at Hack Night. These artifacts were based on our UX findings in the inspiration phase.


Persona Mapping on 2x2 Matrix

We used a 2 x 2 matrix to map new member personas, with ‘skill levels’ and ‘attitudes about civic projects’ as the two axis. We identified four major types of new member personas: learners, helpers, doers, and visionaries. We also mapped out their goals, pain points, and needs in order to be productive at Hack night.



Four major types of personas

Here are the four major types of personas that the group decided to focus on:

  • Learner. Learners come to Code for SF with the goal of developing and practicing a new skill. Their skill level is usually lower, but their motivation to learn is strong. This type of new member needs opportunities to practice, ideally with a mentor.
  • Helper. Helpers are driven to help the community and contribute to social good. They are willing to either contribute their current skills or pick up new skills as long as the project mission is aligned with their social value. What ‘helpers’ need are projects with clear missions and goals, and effective ways for them to contribute.
  • Doer. Doers are highly skilled, and they want to directly apply their skills for social good. However, unlike ‘helpers’, they are not particularly attracted by a social cause or project mission. Instead, ‘doers’ are more interested in directly jumping into a project and picking up a task from the to-do list. What ‘doers’ need is a clear to-do list from projects, and deliverables from the past to feel motivated.
  • Visionary. The most distinctive part about visionary is their motivation to solve a social issue by kick-starting a project at the Hack Night. What they need is guideline, and opportunities for pitching and recruitment.


Journey Mapping and Pain Point Identification

We also created journey maps for the four personas to illustrate their experiences and particularly their pain points at different stages of the Code for SF Hack Night, as well as opportunities for improvement. The UX Research team summarized the pain points and derived four major improvement opportunities.

  • How to physically display Hack Night-related information, such as agenda and floor space, to reduce uncertainty?
  • How to make it easier for new members to approach project teams?
  • How to provide critical project information and help new members identify project teams of interests?
  • How to facilitate matching between new members and project teams based on shared interests and skills?
