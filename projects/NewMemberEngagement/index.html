New Member Engagement at Code for SF Hack Night


Problem Statement

Having noticed the difficulties experienced by new members navigating the Hack Night and seeking a fitting project team to join, as well as the frustrations of existing project teams recruiting talent to reach their goals, the UX Research Group decided to take on the project of improving new member engagement in December 2015.

The New Member Engagement project centers on helping new members to find tractions early on, to join a fitting project team, and ultimately to contribute to project success.


Methods: User-Centered Design

The UX Research Group adopted user-centered design approaches, which include multi-stage problem solving processes in which user needs are given extensive attention at every stage. This is to ensure that new members’ goals and interests are integrated throughout the process of solution development. The group also modified the conventional user-centered design processes to be design thinking-driven and ultra-agile, enabling participants with all backgrounds to contribute productively to New Member Engagement project at the Code for SF Hack Night.


Project History

With new members’ needs in mind, The Research Group moved through three user-centered design phases: inspiration, ideation, and implementation (details see Supplementary at the end of this document).


In the inspiration phase, we identified new members’ pain points and needs at the Hack Night by conducting user interviews and surveys, and drew inspirations upon the identified needs.

In the ideation phase, we created new member personas based on the earlier inspirations we gained from the ideation findings, and empathize with their feelings and pain points at the Hack Night through the exercise of journey mapping . We also invited new members to participate in our ideation brainstorming sessions to generate lots of ideas, aiming to address new member needs with feasibility and desirability solutions.

In the implementation phase, we turned high priority solutions into different formats of prototypes, including physical signage, services, and digital web apps. Through agile user testing and prototype iterations, we are currently in the process of improving our prototypes, and turning our prototypes into full implementations.
