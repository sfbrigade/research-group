Implementation: Signage


Physical signage was designed to address the challenge of first-time new membersâ€™ feeling uncertain about Hack Night location, agenda, and floor space, and to further encourage new members to participate in Code for SF projects.

Here are the steps the UX Research Group took to design signage together with new members.


Sketch and Decide

  • Speedy sketch. We invited new members to join the team to have a speedy sketch session, where participants are instructed to sketch out different signage for under 1-min each, and posted their designs on the wall. The goal was not to individually come up with perfect designs, but to generate as many designs as possible as a group, and to converge on winning ideas in the following steps.

  • Silent critique. Participants silently looked through each design and voted on components they like by drawing dots next to them. By having a silent critique session, individuals had the opportunity to consider various options without the influence from others or authority.

  • Voting. The group discussed about the components we liked one by one, and decided on three sketches per sign for prototyping.



Prototype and Test

  • Prototyping & user testing. We first prototyped the signage with powerpoint/photoshop/illustrator, and modified our prototypes based on feedback collected from user-testing sessions.

  • Live prototyping. We printed out the signage and observed how Code for SF participants use the signage at the hack night. Different from digital web/mobile designs, we learned that the size of the signage, as well as the location and position of the signage, all affected their effectiveness. All of these factors shaped the evolution of our signage designs.
